## What V3 Covers That V1 Did Not

1. **Causal sequence (4 stages):** Impression → Assent → Impulse → Action — the complete mechanism behind every choice. V1 touched on impressions and assent but did not teach the full sequence as a connected system.

2. **Passions taxonomy (25 sub-species):** V1 taught the 4 root passions. V3 teaches all 25 named sub-species with their specific false judgements, enabling precise diagnosis rather than vague categorisation.

3. **Five-step passion diagnostic:** A structured diagnostic sequence for identifying which specific passion is operative, which false judgement drives it, and what the correct judgement would be. V1 had no equivalent.

4. **Eupatheiai (3 rational good feelings):** Joy, rational wish, and rational caution — what replaces passions. V1 mentioned eupatheiai briefly but did not teach them as a system.

5. **The missing fourth asymmetry:** Why no good feeling replaces distress. This is a philosophically significant teaching that V1 did not cover.

6. **Kathekon/katorthoma distinction:** The two-layer action model (appropriate action vs. right action). V1 did not teach this distinction explicitly.

7. **Oikeiosis sequence (5 stages):** The expanding circles of natural affiliation. V1 touched on cosmopolitanism but did not teach the formal 5-stage sequence or the priority rule.

8. **Cicero's 5-question deliberation framework:** A practical decision-making structure from De Officiis. V1 had no equivalent structured decision tool.

9. **Senecan progress grades (3 grades):** A nuanced model of moral progress from Seneca's Epistulae 75. V1 had alignment tiers (numeric score ranges), not philosophical progress grades.

10. **Four progress dimensions:** Reduction of passions, quality of judgement, stability of disposition, expansion of concern. V1 had no multidimensional progress assessment.

11. **Impulse taxonomy (8 types):** From basic intention through moral choice to rational wish. V1 did not distinguish between types of impulse.

12. **Selection vs. desire:** The practical distinction between rationally selecting preferred indifferents and passionately desiring them. V1 did not teach this.

13. **Source citations per entry:** Every V3 journal entry traces to a specific primary source (R7). V1 entries had no source attribution.

---

## What V1 Covered That V3 Handles Differently

1. **Gratitude phase:** V1 Phase 6 was "Living in Gratitude" (7 days). V3 does not have a gratitude phase because gratitude is not a primary concept in the V3 dataset. Gratitude-related ideas are subsumed into joy (eupatheia replacing irrational pleasure) and justice (benevolence as a sub-expression). This reflects R6a: the structure emerged from V3's data, not from V1's layout.

2. **Acceptance phase:** V1 Phase 5 was "Practising Acceptance" (8 days). V3 replaces this with "Right Action in the World" (action theory from action.json). Acceptance concepts are covered through the cosmic framework (Day 6) and the discussion of fate/rational order within the foundations phase, but not as a standalone domain.

3. **Per-virtue scoring:** V1 tagged entries with a single virtue and used per-virtue colour coding as a primary organising principle. V3 retains optional virtue tagging for display purposes but treats it as engagement marker, not a scoring dimension, consistent with the unity thesis (R6b).

4. **Numeric tier system:** V1's completion assessment used alignment tiers (0–100 ranges). V3's completion assessment uses the 4 progress dimensions (qualitative self-assessment) — no numeric scores.

---

## Recommendations

1. **V3 journal serves equivalent function:** It teaches a complete Stoic practice path from foundations through self-examination. The 55-day count is comparable to V1's 56. All core Stoic concepts are covered.

2. **V3 journal is materially more comprehensive:** 13 concept domains are new or substantially expanded compared to V1. The passions taxonomy, causal sequence, action theory, and progress model are all significantly deeper.

3. **V3 journal is better traced:** Every entry carries source file and source citation, enabling R7 compliance verification.

4. **V3 journal is R8c compliant:** All user-facing content is English-only. The page title changed from "Path of the Prokoptos" to "Path of Progress." No Greek or Latin terms appear in the UI.

5. **Existing user data:** Users who have completed entries under V1's 56-day structure will have completion records referencing days 1–56. The transition should handle this gracefully — users who completed V1 should retain their progress, and the curriculum map should adapt. This is a Phase 15 (UX Redesign) concern, not a Phase 4 concern.
