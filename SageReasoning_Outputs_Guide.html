7. Key Findings Summary

These findings come from interpreting 119 journal entries across 12 sections of Clinton's handwritten Stoic journal (October–December 2025).

Proximity Estimate

Deliberate Proficiens Medius
Between habitual and deliberate, trending firmly toward deliberate by journal's end. Can articulate Stoic principles accurately, identifies own passions and false judgements, and shows genuine developmental change across the 55+ day arc. The gap is between intellectual understanding and dispositional stability.

Primary Passion

Philodoxia (love of honour) — 9 of 12 sections
The false judgement that external recognition is necessary for self-worth is the single most persistent passion across the entire journal. Root: epithumia (craving). Core false judgement appears in every form — work reputation, family honour, creative recognition, social belonging.

Primary Causal Breakdown

Synkatathesis (Assent)
The most frequent failure point is giving assent to false impressions — particularly that reputation, respect, and acknowledgement constitute genuine goods. Secondary: horme (impulse) — anger bypasses deliberation. Tertiary: phantasia (impression) — catastrophising imagination.

Strongest Virtue

Phronesis (Wisdom) — Strong
Best philosophical articulation across the journal. Self-authored maxim: "There is no good or bad in nature just true or false." Pass/fail rules for speech. Mean value concept. Comprehensive bias checklist.

Weakest Virtue

Andreia (Courage) — Developing
The gap between knowing the right action and taking it. Over-planning as avoidance. Fear of creative failure. Social withdrawal patterns. But: clear trend of improvement — reaching out to family, shouting replaced with encouragement.

Self-Authored Maxim (Highest Engagement)

"There is no good or bad in nature, just true or false — everything else is just my or their perspective of things which cannot be trusted."
Engagement intensity: 0.95 — the highest across all 119 entries. Maps to Stoic concept: axioma (value proposition). Source: Be Content, Entry 6.
