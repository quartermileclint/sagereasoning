⚠ Agonia (anxiety about future outcomes)
Triggers when: Outcomes depend on other people's decisions, particularly in business contexts, especially when the timeline is uncertain.
Journal evidence: Master Your Feelings (pp. 44-51), Embrace Difficulty (pp. 22-26)
False judgement: "Being left behind is a genuine evil" (it is an indifferent)
Causal stage: Assent given too quickly — impression accepted before examination
Historical resolution: Applying the prohairesis filter; asking "what here is actually within my control?"
⚠ Propeteia (rashness in assent)
Triggers when: Perceived market pressure combined with time constraints; competitive comparison contexts.
Journal evidence: Master Your Thoughts (pp. 33-38), Embrace Difficulty (pp. 20-24)
False judgement: "Speed of response determines outcome" (the quality of the response matters more)
Causal stage: Impulse activated before full deliberation — skipping from impression to action
Historical resolution: Introducing a deliberate pause; writing out the reasoning before acting
⚠ Philodoxia (love of honour/recognition)
Triggers when: Opportunities for public recognition; speaking engagements; feature coverage; partnership offers that emphasise visibility.
Journal evidence: A Virtuous Life (pp. 28-31), Cultivate Wisdom (pp. 72-74)
False judgement: "Being recognised validates the work" (the work's value is independent of recognition)
Causal stage: Value misclassification — treating reputation (preferred indifferent) as genuine good
Historical resolution: Asking "Would I still do this if no one ever knew?" (Cicero's persona test)
