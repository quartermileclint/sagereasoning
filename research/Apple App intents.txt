App intents

App Intents turn apps into tools.
Shortcuts turn tools into workflows.
Siri turns workflows into autonomous behaviour.

Now they power:
	•	Siri (voice + upcoming AI agent)
	•	Spotlight (run actions directly from search)
	•	Shortcuts automation
	•	Widgets / Action Button / system UI
	•	Apple Intelligence workflows

The “API layer” for Apple Intelligence
	•	App Intents are how AI understands your app
	•	They define what actions the AI can take on your behalf
	•	Apple explicitly links them to enabling “enhanced action capabilities of Siri and Apple Intelligence” 

Apple is rebuilding Siri into a system-wide agent that can:
	•	Read context (email, calendar, apps)
	•	Take actions across apps
	•	Chain workflows automatically

This only works because:
➡️ Apps expose structured actions via App Intents

Example:
	•	“Send the doc John sent me yesterday to Slack”
	•	Siri maps:
	•	Mail → find doc
	•	Files → retrieve
	•	Slack → send
(all via App Intents)

3. Turning apps into composable building blocks

With App Intents:
	•	Apps are no longer isolated UIs
	•	They become modular capabilities

Apple literally wants:

Apps = APIs for the OS + AI

Apple has been expanding App Intents with:

• Interactive snippets
	•	Mini UI responses shown in system surfaces
	•	Users can interact without opening the app  ￼

• Spotlight execution
	•	Run app actions directly from search

• AI integration (“Use Model” in Shortcuts)
	•	Combine your app’s actions with LLM reasoning  ￼

• Visual intelligence hooks
	•	Apps can participate in visual AI features


New Apple world
App = capabilities the system calls
AI orchestrates apps
App Intents for the OS

A closed, on-device “tool ecosystem” where every app exposes safe, structured actions.

App Intents are basically:
	•	Apple’s version of tool schemas
	•	The interface layer for agent-driven workflows on-device

If you were building for this ecosystem:
	•	You don’t just build an app
	•	You build:
	•	Intents (actions)
	•	Entities (data models)
	•	Queries (retrieval logic)

One-line summary

App Intents are how apps become “callable tools” for Siri, Spotlight, and Apple’s AI—turning the OS into an agent that can use apps instead of just open them.
