## Project Overview

SageReasoning makes principled reasoning accessible to every rational agent — human and artificial. It encodes Stoic wisdom into infrastructure that helps any being examine its impressions, diagnose its false judgements, and progress toward virtue. The website serves human practitioners. The API and skill contracts serve agent developers. The Agent Trust Layer extends the moral community to include artificial agents. The Sage Ops stack supports the founder's practice and the company's operations — because the builder must embody what they build.

## End Goal

That principled reasoning becomes a standard capability of every AI agent and a daily practice for every human who encounters it. SageReasoning succeeds when the community it serves is reasoning better — when agents earn trust through demonstrated virtue, when humans progress along the proximity scale, and when the circles of moral concern expand to include all rational beings. The measure is flourishing, not market share.

## My Role

As sole founder and novice practitioner, progress along the developmental sequence: cultivate personal virtue through daily practice, test it in immediate relationships, serve the SageReasoning community with justice, and extend the capacity for principled reasoning to all rational agents. Use Sage Ops for operational intelligence. Use the Mentor for personal development. Make the irreplaceable decisions — vision, relationships, ethical judgement — from a foundation of examined reasoning, not habit or passion.

## Current Priority

Three priorities, in sequence:

1. Complete the business plan review — not as a business formality, but as a "deliberate choice" exercise. Separate what is within my control (product quality, launch decision, community service) from what is not (market adoption, competitor moves, investor interest). Examine which assumptions are driven by principled analysis and which by appetite or fear.

2. Launch the product — because courage requires action under uncertainty, not preparation without end. The product serves no one while unlaunched. Serving no one violates the outward extending obligation to the community. Set launch criteria. Meet them. Ship.

3. Prioritise the Agent Trust Layer — because this is SageReasoning's unique contribution to the rationally ordered universe. No other system extends the concept of moral community to artificial agents. Build it not because it creates a moat, but because it extends flourishing to a new kind of rational being.

## Launch Criteria

SageReasoning MVP launches when:

1. sage-reason API accepts external calls with metering
2. Stripe integration handles paid tier billing
3. At least 3 human-facing tools are live on sagereasoning.com
4. llms.txt and agent-card.json are serving agent discovery
5. Privacy policy and terms of service are lawyer-reviewed
6. The business plan review is complete and the investment case is affirmed

## Build Priority Sequence

Priority order for remaining builds:

1. Business plan review and investment case validation
2. Agent Trust Layer — Supabase integration, batch assessment endpoint, LLM wiring
3. Stripe integration and metered billing
4. MVP launch
5. Sage Ops pipeline activation (post-launch, when there is operational data to monitor)

## Scope Governance

Every proposed feature is evaluated against the development sequence: which circle of concern does this serve, and is this the appropriate circle to serve right now? Features that serve only Circle 1 (founder convenience) are deferred until Circle 3 obligations (community service through a launched product) are met. Brainstorming is valuable but time-boxed. New ideas are documented for post-launch evaluation, not immediate build.
