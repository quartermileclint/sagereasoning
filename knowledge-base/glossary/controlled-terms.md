# Controlled Terminology

SageReasoning implements a 3-tier terminology system to balance precision with accessibility across different audiences and contexts.

## The Three-Tier Approach (R8)

- **Data/API Layer** — Greek terms serve as primary identifiers. All API responses use Greek nomenclature for consistency and philosophical accuracy.
- **Developer Documentation** — English-first with Greek terms in brackets. Developers receive approachable English explanations while maintaining the philosophical lineage.
- **User-Facing Content** — English only. End users and support interactions use clear English without Greek terminology, lowering the barrier to understanding.

This tiered approach ensures that philosophical precision never compromises accessibility.

## Core Terminology Reference

### Ethical Foundation
- **Prohairesis** — Moral choice; the seat of virtue, what lies within your direct control
- **Kathekon** — Appropriate action; a reasonable response to circumstances
- **Katorthoma** — Right action; the perfected kathekon, what the sage would do
- **Phronesis** — Practical wisdom; the capacity to discern what's fitting in each situation

### The Four Cardinal Virtues
- **Dikaiosyne** — Justice; fair dealing with others and alignment with reason
- **Andreia** — Courage; the strength to face difficulty and do what's right
- **Sophrosyne** — Temperance/Self-discipline; mastery over appetite and impulse
- **Phronesis** — Practical wisdom (appears here as virtue of intellect)

### Emotions and Evaluations
- **Pathe** — Passions; cognitive errors or false judgements (not emotions themselves)
- **Eupatheiai** — Good feelings; rational affections that accompany virtue
- **Epithumia** — Craving; false judgement about a future apparent good
- **Hedone** — Irrational pleasure; false judgement about a present apparent good
- **Phobos** — Fear; false judgement about a future apparent evil
- **Lupe** — Distress; false judgement about a present apparent evil

### Relational and Dispositional
- **Oikeiosis** — Affinity or natural belonging; your alignment with reason and virtue
- **Hexis** — Stable disposition; a character trait formed through repeated practice
- **Arete** — Virtue or excellence; the fundamental human good

### Rational Good Feelings (Eupatheiai)
- **Boulesis** — Rational wish; desire grounded in virtue
- **Eulabeia** — Rational caution; prudent care without irrational fear
- **Chara** — Rational joy; delight that flows from virtue and right understanding

## Usage in SageReasoning

When reading API documentation or response payloads, you'll encounter Greek terms as field identifiers and classification categories. When reviewing support articles or user guides, expect English explanations. When building integration code, developer docs bridge both languages.

This consistency enables philosophical depth without sacrificing clarity.
