# What R1 Means: No Therapeutic Implication

## The Rule

**R1 (No Therapeutic Implication)**: No data files or user-facing content may present, imply, or be reasonably interpreted as offering therapeutic, psychological, or clinical services.

This is SageReasoning's most important governance rule. It protects users and keeps the service within its proper philosophical scope.

## What Is Allowed

- **Philosophical self-examination**: Exploring decisions through the lens of Stoic philosophy
- **Stoic exercises and practices**: Journaling prompts, reflection questions, dichotomy-of-control worksheets
- **Reasoning evaluation**: Analyzing decision quality using ancient reasoning frameworks
- **Philosophical learning**: Understanding Stoic theory and practice from primary sources
- **Personal growth through philosophy**: Using Stoic thought for self-improvement and resilience

Users exploring self-improvement through Stoic philosophy are welcome. Philosophy has always been a practical discipline for human flourishing.

## What Is Not Allowed

Framing SageReasoning as a substitute for professional mental health care. We do not:

- **Diagnose psychological conditions**: While we identify "passions" (false judgements in the Stoic technical sense), these are not clinical diagnoses
- **Prescribe treatment**: Our recommendations are frameworks for reflection, never medical or psychological protocols
- **Replace therapy**: Users with clinical concerns should consult qualified healthcare providers, not API evaluations
- **Claim therapeutic benefit**: We evaluate reasoning quality, not mental health outcomes

## Key Distinctions: Stoic Language vs. Clinical Language

| Stoic Concept | Not A Clinical Concept |
|---|---|
| Passion (false judgement) | Psychological diagnosis |
| Dichotomy of control | Coping mechanism |
| Virtue practice | Clinical intervention |
| Oikeiosis (nature alignment) | Therapeutic alignment |
| Premeditatio malorum (negative visualization) | Rumination or catastrophizing |

When a user detects a "passion" (cognitive error) in SageReasoning, they've identified a logical false judgement about what is good or bad—not a symptom of illness.

## Why R1 Matters

1. **Legal protection**: SageReasoning is not licensed to provide mental health services
2. **User safety**: Relying on an API instead of professional help for clinical issues could cause harm
3. **Philosophical integrity**: Stoicism is a philosophy of life, not a clinical framework
4. **Clear scope**: Users know what they're getting—philosophy, not healthcare

## What Should I Tell Users?

If a customer asks whether SageReasoning can help with anxiety, depression, trauma, or other clinical concerns:

- Affirm that philosophy can be personally meaningful
- Clarify that SageReasoning is a reasoning evaluation tool, not therapy
- Recommend they consult a mental health professional if they have clinical concerns
- Offer SageReasoning as a supplementary philosophical practice, not a substitute

Example response: *"SageReasoning helps you examine your reasoning through Stoic philosophy. Many people find philosophy meaningful for personal growth. However, if you're experiencing clinical anxiety or depression, please speak with a qualified therapist or physician. SageReasoning is a tool for philosophical practice, not a treatment."*

## Red Flags in Customer Conversations

If a customer describes:
- Suicidal ideation, self-harm, or substance abuse → Direct them to crisis resources immediately
- Ongoing clinical symptoms (panic attacks, severe depression, etc.) → Recommend professional help
- Using SageReasoning specifically to treat a diagnosed condition → Gently clarify our scope
- Replacing medication or therapy with SageReasoning → Strongly recommend professional consultation

These situations fall outside R1's allowance and require clear guidance to professional resources.
